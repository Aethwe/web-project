Audio

The story takes place in a zombie apocalypse. A man is walking outside before he pauses to shoot at a zombie. The sound of the gunshot alerts a horde of zombies in his direction, so he takes off running to a nearby shelter and slams the door behind him. The zombies bang at the door, then break it to enter the shelter, killing the man inside. I really like zombie media growing up, and was inspired by the games I would play. I used 13 sounds in total and adjusted the speed of certain clips to better match the situation. The most challenging part of my project was figuring out how to place clips to make them sound seamless. This required a lot of trial and error.

Image

I chose these images because they matched the beach background's vibe, and I added a picture of my brother to give the image a personal feel. I combined 5 images. My sources of inspiration were beach weddings and dates. Old-school Photoshop was more challenging to brush out the subject from the background, while AI-assisted made the process way easier with a push of a button. My AI-prompted image did not turn out as I expected, but it was able to capture the feeling of the original image. The most challenging part of the project was cutting the subject out of the background, and I solved it with a lot of time and patience.

Animation

I decided to animate the emotion of betrayal through shapes, the movement of the shapes, and using text as dialogue between the shapes. This tells a story of two brothers depicted as squares, being forced to fight each other. However, the yellow brother tells the green one that he does not want to fight. Once the yellow square’s guard is lowered, the green charges towards it and pushes the yellow off the platform. This leaves the yellow square feeling betrayed. My main inspirations were 2d fighting games such as Street Fighter or Mortal Kombat. My animation changed from storyboard to final project because the animation ended up being longer than expected. I was able to use all four transform properties: position, rotation, scale, and opacity. The most challenging part of the project was syncing the keyframes to the specific position I wanted the objects to move to. I solved this with a lot of trial and error.

3D

I made a 3D model of a pencil. I was inspired by the pencil sitting next to me while brainstorming for ideas. The intended use for my model is in an animation. I chose these specific shapes because they could be easily morphed into a pencil. I used holes in a cylinder to cut out the angular sides of a pencil. The most challenging part of the project was lining all the shapes up and making them all fit together.
